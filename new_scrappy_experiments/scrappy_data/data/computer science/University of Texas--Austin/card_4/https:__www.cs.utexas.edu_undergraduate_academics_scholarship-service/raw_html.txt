Program</h1>\n            \n</div>\n\n\n<!-- Begin main region 1 -->\n  <div id=\"main\">\n\t\n    <div id=\"content\" class=\"column\" role=\"main\">\n\n                        \n\n\n<article class=\"node-47224 node node-page view-mode-full clearfix\" about=\"/undergraduate/academics/scholarship-service\" typeof=\"foaf:Document\">\n\n      <header>\n                  <span property=\"dc:title\" content=\"INFOSEC Scholarship for Service (SFS) Program\" class=\"rdf-meta element-hidden\"></span><span property=\"sioc:num_replies\" content=\"0\" datatype=\"xsd:integer\" class=\"rdf-meta element-hidden\"></span>\n      \n          </header>\n  \n  <div class=\"field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden\"><div class=\"field-items\"><div class=\"field-item even\" property=\"content:encoded\"><p>UTCS has a grant from the National Science Foundation (NSF) to support students pursuing the <a href=\"https://www.cs.utexas.edu/concentrations/cybersecurity\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"nofollow\">Cybersecurity&nbsp;Concentration&nbsp;</a>and have a desire to use the skills gained through the&nbsp;program by working as an information assurance professional for a government entity.&nbsp; The <a href=\"https://www.sfs.opm.gov/\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"nofollow\">CyberCorps: Scholarship for Service (SFS) program</a> provides extensive financial support and other educational opportunities to student pursuing the Cybersecurity&nbsp;Concentration.&nbsp; Upon graduation, participating students work for a federal, state, tribal, or local government agency in a position focused on cybersecurity.</p>\n<h2>Program Overview</h2>\n<p>Scholarship for Service (SFS) is a unique program designed to increase and strengthen the cadre of information assurance professionals that protect the government's critical information infrastructure.&nbsp; The program provides generous scholarships that cover many of the typical expenses students incur, such as tuition and fees, books, healthcare, and housing.&nbsp; In return, participating students work for a federal or other government agency after graduation; the period of government service matches the scholarship support period.&nbsp;</p>\n<p>The INFOSEC SFS program integrates preparation for public service careers into the academic offerings and experiences provided to students in the&nbsp;program.&nbsp; Students pursuing an undergraduate degree in CS as well as the Cybersecurity&nbsp;Concentration&nbsp;may participate in SFS during their final two years as a Texas Computer Science&nbsp;student. The goals of this&nbsp;program are to:</p>\n<ol><li>Prepare students with the academic knowledge to be successful cybersecurity professionals.</li>\n<li>Provide students with the career development skills to be successful public servants.</li>\n<li>Increase student involvement in cybersecurity research.</li>\n<li>Place all SFS program participants in summer internships and full-time jobs in information assurance in government agencies.</li>\n</ol><h2>Benefits to Participating Students</h2>\n<p>Participating undergraduates receive the following each year for up to two years:</p>\n<ul><li>Tuition and fees for up to 12 credit hours for fall and spring semesters</li>\n<li>$24,500 stipend per year</li>\n<li>$3,000 health insurance reimbursement allowance</li>\n<li>$2,000 for textbooks</li>\n<li>$4,000 professional development allowance (primarily to participate in a federal agency career fair)</li>\n</ul><h2>Program Eligibility</h2>\n<p>To apply for the program, you must:</p>\n<ul><li>Be a CS major (junior or senior class standing)</li>\n<li>Have a minimum&nbsp;GPA of 3.0</li>\n<li>Be a full time student</li>\n<li>Be a United States citizen or permanent resident&nbsp;</li>\n<li>Meet criteria for employment with the federal government</li>\n<li>Be able to receive a federal government security clearance</li>\n<li>Complete the application and application process</li>\n</ul><p>Students who do not meet these requirements but have a strong desire to participate may apply and request special consideration.</p>\n<h2>Program Requirements</h2>\n<p>As a student in the program, you will:</p>\n<ul><li>Participate in the INFOSEC SFS annual kick-off</li>\n<li>Be paired with a UTCS faculty member affiliated with the <a href=\"http://www.cias.utexas.edu/index.htm\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"nofollow\">Center for Information Assurance and Security (CIAS)</a></li>\n<li>Create annual personal goals for academic and career development, and report progress toward those goals monthly</li>\n<li>Participate in a research team affiliated with a CIAS faculty member</li>\n<li>Present team or individual research at a university forum</li>\n<li>Attend at least three research presentations in the CIAS lecture series, and participate in Security Day activities</li>\n<li>Attend two career workshops focused on gaining and being successful in government employment</li>\n<li>Attend a site visit at a government agency in Texas</li>\n<li>Attend two on-campus and one national career fair for government agency hiring</li>\n<li>Serve as a mentor to an incoming student</li>\n</ul><p>In addition, you must:</p>\n<ul><li>Maintain an undergraduate GPA of 3.0 or above</li>\n<li>Complete your UTCS studies within two years of starting the SFS program</li>\n<li>Complete the Cybersecurity&nbsp;Concentration&nbsp;coursework within two years of starting the SFS program</li>\n<li>Be enrolled full time during the fall and spring semesters</li>\n<li>Apply for and be hired as an intern with a government agency the summer prior to your expected graduation (between your junior and senior year)</li>\n<li>Post and maintain a resume on the Office of Personnel Management online system</li>\n</ul><h2>How to Apply&nbsp;</h2>\n<p>&nbsp;To apply, please submit:</p>\n<ul><li>&nbsp;Your resume</li>\n<li>A personal statement of purpose describing your career plans in information assurance and your commitment to public service</li>\n<li>The names of two&nbsp;UTCS&nbsp;faculty members&nbsp;we can contact for an academic reference</li>\n</ul><p>*The UT Austin Department of Computer Science is not currently accepting applications for the SFS program. Please check back in Spring 2020.&nbsp;</p>\n<p>FAQs</p>\n<ol><li><strong>Can I apply before I'm admitted to UT Austin?&nbsp; </strong>No, only students admitted to and enrolled in the UT Austin Department of Computer Science (UTCS) may apply.</li>\n<li><strong>When do I apply?</strong>&nbsp; If you are a UTCS student, you should apply the semester before you begin your junior or senior year.&nbsp;</li>\n<li><strong>&nbsp;Can students in the five-year master's program participate in SFS? &nbsp;</strong>Not at this time.</li>\n<li><strong>Can traditional master's students or PhD students participate?&nbsp; </strong>No, the program is only available to undergraduates.</li>\n<li><strong>Do I have to sign a contract?&nbsp; </strong>Yes, you will sign a written service agreement that details your responsibilities.<strong> </strong></li>\n<li><strong>Am I required to work for two years if I earn my degree in less than two years?</strong>&nbsp; No, the work requirement is a one-for-one match between the time you are supported and the time you are expected to work, but you must work a minimum of one year.<strong>&nbsp;</strong></li>\n<li><strong>Does an internship or employment at a local government agency fulfill the work requirement?</strong>&nbsp; Yes, you may work for federal, state, or local government entities.<strong>&nbsp;</strong></li>\n<li><strong>Can I do my internship or be employed by a national laboratory?</strong>&nbsp; Yes, a limited number of students may be placed in National Laboratories and Federally Funded Research and Development Centers (FFRDCs).</li>\n<li><strong>Can I fulfill my internship or employment responsibility by working at a government contractor?</strong>&nbsp; No, you must work at a federal, state, local, or tribal government organization, an independent agency, government corporation, commission, quasi-official agency, national laboratory, or federally funded research development center.</li>\n<li><strong>Do I have to work in the area of cybersecurity?</strong>&nbsp; Yes, you must be employed at a government agency in an information assurance position.</li>\n<li><strong>Am I guaranteed a permanent position with a federal agency?</strong>&nbsp; No, you are responsible for your own job search, but will receive support in that search through events and services such as a national career fair and a database of participants' resumes.</li>\n<li><strong>What is the starting salary for someone graduating from this program?</strong>&nbsp; Federal employees with a bachelor's degree typically begin their employment at the GS-7 level and above, and those with a master's degree may be appointed at the GS-9 level.&nbsp; Higher civil service levels and steps may be applicable depending on many factors.&nbsp; The hiring agency makes the determination about the <a href=\"https://www.opm.gov/policy-data-oversight/pay-leave/salaries-wages/#url=2013\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"nofollow\">civil service step and pay grade</a>.&nbsp; Federal pay rates vary depending on the geographic location of the appointment, and special pay rates have been created for federal IT-related jobs.</li>\n<li><strong>What happens if I do not accept an offer and am not placed?&nbsp; </strong>If you decline a reasonable offer and do not find a position to meet your obligation you could be required to pay back the monies received through the scholarship.</li>\n<li><strong>What happens if I leave my job before I've served the time required?</strong>&nbsp; You must repay a prorated amount equivalent to the length of the period not served.&nbsp;</li>\n<li><strong>How can I get additional information? </strong>The U.S. Department of Personnel Management has an <a href=\"https://www.sfs.opm.gov/StudFAQ.aspx\" target=\"_blank\" rel=\"nofollow\">extensive list of FAQs</a> related to the SFS program that you may find helpful.&nbsp; Or, contact a representative of UTCS listed below.</li>\n</ol><h2>UTCS SFS Contacts</h2>\n<p>Michaela Cicero, staff contact<br><a href=\"mailto:mcicero@cs.utexas.edu\">mcicero@cs.utexas.edu</a><br>\n512.471.9517</p>\n<p>Bill Young, PhD, faculty contact<br><a href=\"mailto:byoung@cs.utexas.edu\">byoung@cs.utexas.edu</a><br>\n512.471.9782</p>\n</div></div></div>\n  \n  \n</article>\n          </div>\n\n\n    \n          <aside
